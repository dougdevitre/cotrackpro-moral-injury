# What is this? (the simple version)

*A plain-language guide to the CoTrackPro Moral Injury project. For the technical README, see
[README.md](README.md). You can try the live app at
[morality.cotrackpro.com](https://morality.cotrackpro.com/).*

> **If you or someone you know is in crisis or thinking about self-harm, please reach out now.**
> In the U.S., call or text **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), any hour. Outside the U.S.,
> contact your local crisis line or a trusted clinician. This website is a place to reflect —
> it is not an emergency service.

## What is this?

This is a website that helps grown-ups who work with families in court — like lawyers, judges, and
counselors — take care of their own feelings.

Their jobs can be really hard. Sometimes they see sad things, or they have to do something that
doesn't feel right, even when it's their job. Over time, that can weigh on a person's heart. This
website gives them a quiet, private place to stop and check in with themselves, and it gives them
ideas for feeling better.

It is **not** a doctor and it does **not** give anyone a grade or say if they are "good" or "bad."
It just helps people notice how they feel and points them toward help.

## Is it private?

Yes. Almost everything you do here stays on your own computer. Nothing gets sent anywhere unless
you choose to save or print it yourself. The one exception is a "wall" where people can share a
promise if they want to — but that part is turned off until someone sets it up on purpose.

## What can you do on it?

Think of it like a little clubhouse with different rooms. You pick the room you want:

- **Reflect** — Answer some simple questions about your work. The website shows you how heavy
  things feel right now, and where you might want a little support.
- **Course** — A lesson you can take to learn about this stuff. If you finish, you get a
  certificate, kind of like when you finish a class at school.
- **Decide** — Stuck on a hard choice? This room asks you a few good questions to help you figure
  out the right thing to do.
- **Practice** — Make little "when this happens, I will do that" plans so good habits become easy.
- **Commit** — Make a promise to yourself to take care of your heart, and print it out.
- **Encourage** — Keep a list of the good things you did. On tough days, you can look back and
  smile.
- **Share** — Make a nice picture with a kind message on it that you can share with friends.
- **Standards** — A simple list of the rules these workers are supposed to follow.
- **The long view** — Shows how one small thing a grown-up does can help or hurt a kid for a long
  time — and what the grown-up can do to help.
- **Leaders** — For the bosses. It helps them check if their team feels safe and supported.
- **Calculator** — Shows, in numbers, how much stress can cost — and how much you gain by taking
  care of it.
- **Pledge wall** — A place to put up one promise for everyone to see (only if it's turned on).

## How do you run it?

You need a helper program called **Node** (version 20 or newer) on your computer. Then open the
folder and type these:

```bash
npm install     # get the pieces the website needs
npm run dev      # start the website on your computer so you can look at it
npm run test     # make sure everything still works right
npm run build    # package it up so it's ready to go online
```
